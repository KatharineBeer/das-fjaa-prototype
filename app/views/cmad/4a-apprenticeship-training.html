How your apprenticeship will be delivered

Your apprenticeship will be delivered in two parts:

  • a training period where you may move between different placements
  • an end-point assessment

Your training period

Your flexi-job apprenticeship agency will arrange placements where you will gain hands-on experience in your chosen occupation. At your placement you will be offered training and supervision to help you develop the skills you need for the role.

Each placement will help you develop some of the skills required for your apprenticeship.

Your training provider is responsible for your off-the-job training. Your off-the-job training will be at least 20% of your normal working hours spent learning new knowledge, skills and behaviours for your apprenticeship. Your off-the-job training will be managed by your training provider.

Your training plan is agreed between you and your training provider. The plan will set out a training schedule for your off-the-job training. Your training plan will include:

  • the delivery of the knowledge, skills and behaviours required for your apprenticeship
  • how and when your off-the-job training will be delivered
  • English and maths qualifications, if you have not already achieved these
  • regular progress reviews with your flexi-job apprenticeship agency and training provider

Your end-point assessment

You'll be assessed by an independent end-point assessor on the knowledge, skills and behaviours that you've learnt during your apprenticeship to confirm you're occupationally competent.

You will agree when you're ready for your end-point assessment in your progress reviews with your flexi-job apprenticeship agency and training provider.

Your flexi-job apprenticeship agency and training provider will tell you what to expect and when the assessment will take place.
